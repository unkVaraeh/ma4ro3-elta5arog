Welcome to Alexandria

Alexandria is a historic city on Egypt's Mediterranean coast, founded by Alexander the Great in 331 BC. It was once a major center of culture, science, and learning, home to the ancient Library of Alexandria. Today, it blends ancient landmarks with modern life, remaining an important cultural and tourist destination.

Where to go in Alexandria

Library of Alexandria

The Bibliotheca Alexandrina, founded in the 3rd century BC in Alexandria, was a major center of knowledge, housing hundreds of thousands of manuscripts. It was gradually destroyed by fires and conflicts, with significant losses occurring in 48 BC. The modern library, reopened in 2002, is now a global cultural hub with vast collections, research centers, and a museum.

Qaitbay Citadel

Qaitbay Citadel in Alexandria is a historic fortress built in the 15th century by Sultan Qaitbay on the site of the ancient Pharos Lighthouse. It served as a defense structure against naval invasions and is a prime example of medieval military architecture. Today, it is a popular tourist destination, offering stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea.

Pompey's Pillar

The Pompey’s Pillar in Alexandria is a historic ancient Roman column, standing at 25 meters tall. It was built in the 3rd century AD in honor of Emperor Diocletian, though its name is a misnomer as it has no connection to Pompey. The column is a major archaeological landmark, offering a glimpse into Alexandria's Roman past.

Aquarium Museum

The Aquarium Museum in Alexandria is a unique museum showcasing marine life from the Mediterranean and Red Seas. It houses various species of fish, invertebrates, and other aquatic creatures in well-maintained tanks. The museum offers visitors an educational experience about marine biodiversity and the ecosystem of the region.

St. Mark's Coptic Orthodox Cathedral

St. Mark's Coptic Orthodox Cathedral in Alexandria is one of the oldest and most important churches in Egypt, founded in the 4th century. It is named after St. Mark the Evangelist, who is believed to have founded the Church of Alexandria. The cathedral is a significant religious and historical landmark for the Coptic Orthodox community.

Roman Theature

The Roman Theater in Alexandria is an ancient structure dating back to the 2nd century AD, located in the Kom El-Dikka area. It was used for performances, including plays and concerts, and could accommodate up to 800 spectators. The theater is well-preserved and is a significant archaeological site, showcasing Roman architecture and culture in Egypt.

Stanley Bridge

Stanley Bridge in Alexandria is an iconic landmark that spans the Stanley Bay, connecting the eastern and western parts of the city. Built in 2001, it features distinctive architectural design with two large, ornate towers at each end. The bridge is a popular spot for locals and tourists, offering scenic views of the Mediterranean coastline.
