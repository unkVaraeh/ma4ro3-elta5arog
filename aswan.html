Welcome to Aswan

Aswan is a historic city in southern Egypt, known for its stunning Nile River views and ancient monuments. It's famous for the Aswan High Dam, which regulates the Nile's flow, and the nearby Philae Temple dedicated to the goddess Isis. The city is also a gateway to Nubian culture and offers a mix of natural beauty and archaeological significance.

Where to go in Aswan

The Amada Museum

The Amada Temple is an ancient Egyptian temple located in Nubia, near Aswan. It was built during the 18th Dynasty by Pharaoh Thutmose III and later expanded by Ramses II. The temple is dedicated to the gods Amun, Ra-Horakhty, and the deified Thutmose III, and it is one of the best-preserved temples in Nubia, showcasing remarkable wall reliefs and inscriptions.

Nubia

Nubia is a historical region located along the Nile River, stretching across southern Egypt and northern Sudan. It was home to several ancient kingdoms, such as Ta-Seti and Kush, which thrived in art, culture, and architecture. Today, Nubia is known for its rich cultural heritage, unique language, and distinctive Nubian villages, as well as the relocation of monuments like Abu Simbel due to the construction of the Aswan High Dam.

Philae Temple

The Philae Temple is an ancient Egyptian temple complex located on Philae Island in the Nile River, near Aswan. Dedicated to the goddess Isis, it was originally constructed during the Ptolemaic period and later expanded by the Romans. Due to the construction of the Aswan High Dam, the temple was relocated to Agilkia Island in the 1960s to preserve it from rising waters.

Nubian Museum

The Nubian Museum in Aswan is dedicated to preserving and showcasing the rich history and culture of Nubia. It features a vast collection of artifacts, including ancient sculptures, pottery, and jewelry, as well as exhibits on Nubian architecture and traditions. The museum plays a key role in highlighting the impact of the Nubian civilization, particularly in the context of the construction of the Aswan High Dam.

Tabia Mosque

The Mosque of Al-Tabia is a historic mosque located in Aswan, Egypt. It was built in the 19th century during the reign of Muhammad Ali Pasha, featuring a distinctive Ottoman architectural style. The mosque is an important religious and cultural landmark in the city, known for its impressive dome and minaret, offering a beautiful view of the Nile River.

Elephantine Island

Philae Island is a small island in the Nile River near Aswan, Egypt, known for its rich archaeological significance. It was home to the Philae Temple complex, primarily dedicated to the goddess Isis. Due to the construction of the Aswan High Dam, the temple was relocated to nearby Agilkia Island in the 1960s to protect it from flooding, making Philae Island a key site in Egypt's preservation efforts.

Abu Simple

Abu Simbel is a famous archaeological site in southern Egypt, known for its two massive temples built by Pharaoh Ramses II. The larger temple is dedicated to Ramses II himself, while the smaller one is dedicated to his wife, Queen Nefertari, and the goddess Hathor. To protect them from flooding caused by the Aswan High Dam, the temples were carefully relocated to a higher site in the 1960s, making them an engineering marvel.
