where to go in cairo

pyramids

The Pyramids of Giza, ancient wonders of the world, stand majestically on the outskirts of Cairo. These monumental structures, built as tombs for the Pharaohs, symbolize the grandeur and mystery of ancient Egyptian civilization. The Great Pyramid of Khufu, the largest, is a testament to the architectural ingenuity of the time.

Egyptian Museum

The Egyptian Museum in Cairo, located in Tahrir Square, houses the world’s largest collection of Pharaonic antiquities. Its vast halls are filled with treasures, including the golden mask of Tutankhamun and countless artifacts that narrate the rich history and cultural heritage of ancient Egypt.

Citadel of Saladin

The Citadel of Saladin, a medieval fortress perched on the Mokattam Hills, offers breathtaking panoramic views of Cairo. Built by Saladin in the 12th century, it houses the magnificent Mosque of Muhammad Ali, a symbol of Ottoman architecture and a key historical site

Khan El Khalili

Khan El Khalili, a vibrant and bustling bazaar in the heart of Cairo, is a treasure trove of traditional crafts, jewelry, spices, and souvenirs. Established in the 14th century, it retains its historic charm and offers a unique glimpse into Cairo’s rich cultural tapestry.

Sultan Hassan Mosque

Sultan Hassan Mosque, an architectural masterpiece in Cairo, exemplifies the grandeur of Mamluk architecture. Built in the 14th century, its intricate designs, towering minarets, and vast prayer hall reflect the artistic and spiritual aspirations of the era.

Al-Azhar Park

Al-Azhar Park, a lush green oasis in Cairo, offers stunning views of the city and a peaceful retreat from the urban hustle. Developed on a former landfill, it features beautifully landscaped gardens, fountains, and historic sites, making it a perfect spot for relaxation and leisure.

The Cairo Tower

The Cairo Tower, standing 187 meters tall on Gezira Island, offers a panoramic view of the city. This iconic structure, inspired by the lotus flower, provides visitors with a unique perspective of Cairo’s skyline and a revolving restaurant at the top.
