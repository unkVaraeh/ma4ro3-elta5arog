Welcome to Giza

Giza, located near Cairo, is home to the famous Giza Plateau, which hosts the iconic Great Pyramids and the Sphinx. These ancient structures, built during Egypt's Old Kingdom, are among the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. Giza remains a key archaeological and tourist site, showcasing Egypt's rich history and architectural achievements..

Where to go in Giza

The Pyramids

The Pyramids of Giza, ancient wonders of the world, stand majestically on the outskirts of Cairo. These monumental structures, built as tombs for the Pharaohs, symbolize the grandeur and mystery of ancient Egyptian civilization. The Great Pyramid of Khufu, the largest, is a testament to the architectural ingenuity of the time.

The Pharaonic ship

The Pharaonic Ship, often referred to as a "solar boat," is an ancient Egyptian vessel designed for the afterlife, believed to carry the pharaohs' souls to the gods. One of the most famous examples is the boat found near the Great Pyramid of Giza, dating back to around 2500 BCE. These boats were typically made of cedar wood and meticulously preserved to ensure the pharaoh's journey in the afterlife.

Mit Rahina Museum

The Mit Rahina Museum, located near the ancient city of Memphis, Egypt, showcases a collection of artifacts from the Pharaonic period. It is famous for housing the massive statue of Ramses II, one of the largest sculptures of the pharaoh. The museum also features relics from the Old Kingdom, offering valuable insights into Egypt's history and culture.

Valley of the Whales

Wadi Al-Hitan, or Valley of the Whales, is a UNESCO World Heritage site located in Egypt's Western Desert. It is famous for its rich fossil records, particularly the well-preserved remains of ancient whales and marine life, dating back over 40 million years. The site provides important insights into the evolution of whales from land-dwelling mammals to marine creatures.

Mastaba Of Meruraka

The Mastaba of Mereruka, located in Saqqara, is one of the largest and most elaborate tombs of the Old Kingdom. Built for Mereruka, the vizier during the 6th Dynasty, it features intricate carvings and reliefs depicting daily life and rituals. The tomb offers significant insights into the art, culture, and administration of ancient Egypt.

The Sphinx

The Sphinx, located in Giza, Egypt, is a massive limestone statue with the body of a lion and the head of a human, believed to represent Pharaoh Khafre. It dates back to the 4th Dynasty of the Old Kingdom, around 2500 BCE. The Sphinx remains a symbol of ancient Egyptian power and mystery, attracting millions of visitors each year.

Ramses II

Ramesses II, also known as Ramesses the Great, was the third pharaoh of Egypt's 19th Dynasty and one of the most powerful rulers in ancient Egyptian history. He reigned for 66 years, from 1279 to 1213 BCE, and is known for his military victories, extensive building projects, and numerous statues. Ramesses II's legacy is marked by his role in securing Egypt's power and wealth, as well as his monumental architectural achievements, including the temples of Abu Simbel.
